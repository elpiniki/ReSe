E²SHI Fellowships

The E²SHI Fellowship programs provides doctoral students with the opportunity to build research or teaching experience, while at the same time developing interdisciplinary expertise and breadth needed to address critical issues related to environmental sustainability, including energy challenges and health impacts. Fellowships are ordinarily granted for one academic year.

E²SHI currently offers four Fellowships for Johns Hopkins doctoral students focusing on one of E²SHI’s areas (environment, energy supply and use, economic and ecological sustainability, or their relationship to public health):

E²SHI Fellowships provide up to $25,000 for research that crosses disciplinary boundaries and would lead to doctoral dissertation research.

Gordon Croft Fellowships provide up to $5,000 to graduate students in the Whiting School of Engineering focusing on one of E²SHI’s related areas.

E²SHI Teaching Fellowships provide support for doctoral students to gain teaching experience by offering undergraduate or lower-level graduate courses at Johns Hopkins University.

 

Request for Applications: 2014-2015 E²SHI Fellowships

We are no longer accepting applications for 2014-2015 fellowships. We anticipate announcing the Request for Applications for the next round in late fall 2014. For information on the fellowship program and eligibility requirements, download the Fellowship Request for Application (RFA) from the last round as a reference.
