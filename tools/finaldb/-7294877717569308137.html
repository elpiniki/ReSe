Get Involved in the Community

Check out the student groups active on campus and in the community, and find out opportunities to help create a sustainable future.

Hopkins Partnership for Efficient Transportation

HPET aims to make JHU a leader in the world of sustainable transportation by assisting schools in moving away from gasoline to cleaner fuels.
Visit Site

Alliance for Clean Water

ACWa takes Hopkins students out into the community where they can learn about and further the vision for universal access to clean water.
Visit Site

ECO-Reps

A selective program for JHU freshmen, Reps work together to develop, design, and implement campus greening projects while promoting sustainability.
Visit Site

Real Food Hopkins

Part of the national Real Food Challenge movement, Real Food Hopkins aims to bring local, sustainable, and fair food to JHU and the Baltimore area.
Visit Site

Hopkins GIS Club

Learn about Geographical Information Systems (GIS) and how to apply it within your own work and/or major.
Visit Site

JHU Engineers Without Borders

The JHU chapter of Engineers Without Borders welcomes students, professors and professionals to building a better world, one community at a time.
Visit Site

Graduate Environmental Network

The JHU Graduate Environmental Network is open to graduate students, faculty and staff interested in sustainability on and off the campus.
Visit Site

Net Impact Johns Hopkins Carey Business School

The Net Impact chapter at Hopkins provides education on socially sound, economically just, & environmentally sustainable business practices.
Visit Site

LIFE: Leadership Initiative for the Environment

The Hopkins School of Medicine group promotes environmental stewardship, education, and environmentally supportive research policies.
Visit Site

Environment Maryland

Environment Maryland is a citizen-based environmental advocacy organization that works to protect Maryland's clean air, clean water, and open space.
Visit Site

Campus Kitchens

Based on the premise that waste is wrong, Campus Kitchens cook up donations of surplus food into nutritious meals for hungry Baltimore residents.
Visit Site

SHIP: Sustainable Hopkins Infrastructure Program

SHIP identifies and evaluates opportunities on Homewood campus to reduce water and electricity use and hosts the Green Idea Generator.
