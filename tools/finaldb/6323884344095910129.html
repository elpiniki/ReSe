Research

 I am interested in the use of randomness in computation, complexity theory, distributed computing, cryptography and theory of computation in general. I obtained my Ph.D. degree from University of Texas at Austin in 2011, under the supervison of David Zuckerman. After that I was a Simons Postdoctoral Fellow at University of Washington. Previously I worked a little on quantum computing and human computer interaction.

New: I am looking for talented students who love to work in theoretical computer science. If you are interested in working with me, or just want to know more about my research, please send me an email or come talk to me.
