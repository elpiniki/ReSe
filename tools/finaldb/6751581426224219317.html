E²SHI Director Files Support for EPA’s Cross-State Air Pollution Rule

The United States Environmental Protection Agency (USEPA) proposed Cross-State Air Pollution Rule (CSAPR) to tighten caps on the amount of sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxide emissions by electric power plants in in the eastern U.S. Such pollution drifts across state borders and the resulting particulate pollution and smog harms human health as well as ecosystems. However, the rule was struck down in US District Court, and that decision is now being appealed by USEPA to the Supreme Court. 

On September 11, 2013, E²SHI Director Ben Hobbs, along with other electricity experts from Stanford University and the University of California, submitted a policy brief in support of USEPA's petition. They argued that since neither air pollution nor electricity respect state boundaries, USEPA's proposed approach to regulating pollution - through tight caps on regional emissions - is most compatible with how electricity markets are run. A state-by-state approach could frustrate the pollution reduction goals and increase power costs unnecessarily. Several other industrial, environmental, and public interest groups also submitted supporting briefs.
