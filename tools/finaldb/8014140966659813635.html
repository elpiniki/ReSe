Ultra-Compact Silicon Carbon Based Level-2 Charger for Electric Vehicles

The research group in PEHREL is investigating an ultra-light and efficient isolated Level-2 on-board charger topology, targeting a total weight less than 2.5 kg at 7.6kW with unity power factor charging.  The proposed topology is a novel power electronic structure in which losses are minimized by using zero-voltage-switching interleaved power factor correction stage followed by a high frequency link isolated resonant converter. The design, optimization, and testing of the advanced charger unit is being explored. To further increase the switching and system frequency, use of emerging SiC power FETs are being explored through collaboration with CoolCAD Electronics LLC. The expected outcome is a lighter, more efficient on-board charger, with a power density four times higher than that of the commercially available chargers.

Source of Funding: Maryland Industrial Partnership Program and the CoolCAD Electronics LLC.
